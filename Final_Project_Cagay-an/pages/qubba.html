Qubba

A qubba, also transliterated as ḳubba, kubbet and koubba, is a cupola or domed structure, typically a tomb or shrine in Islamic architecture. In many regions, such as North Africa, the term qubba commonly the tomb of a local wali or marabout, and usually consists of a chamber covered by a dome or pyramidal cupola.

In Islamic Architecture, most of the Mosque has one or more domes, it is called Qubba in Arabic. According to the article, “The dome appears to be a general symbol, signifying power, the royal city, the focal point of assembly; it can therefore serve both religious and secular purposes. Its outward visible appearance does not truly help us to understand, interpret or identify any building.

In the Islamic building tradition, the convoluted dome was often used. The dome is made of concave sections, from the appearance it looks like a pumpkin. The dome, in Islam, represents the vault of heaven in the same way as the garden prefigures Paradise.

The first domed was the Dome of the Rock in Jerusalem built by the Arabs(691) . Timber was used in the Dome of the Rock which made the structure lighter and flexible but is suggested to be covered with copper to protect it from the weather.
